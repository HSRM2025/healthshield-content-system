# Rubric

Pass if all are true:

- Subject line is 50 characters or fewer and gives a clear benefit or reason to open
- Preview text adds useful detail and does not repeat the subject line
- Each email has exactly one primary CTA
- CTA button text is clear and action-led
- Body copy is easy to scan, with short paragraphs and clear sections
- Opening lines make the topic and relevance clear within the first two sentences
- Send timing or trigger is stated for each email in a sequence, or for the monthly nurture send where relevant
- Sequence emails show clear progression and do not repeat the same message without a reason
- The email is clearly adapted for the intended audience: prospect, broker, or customer
- Monthly nurture emails have a clear monthly theme, seasonal hook, or topical reason for sending
- The featured resource is named and briefly explained
- Broker emails clearly frame the resource as something to share with clients
- Customer emails reflect the existing relationship and added value of Health Shield support
- Health-related language stays general, educational, and non-diagnostic
- Claims are careful and supported, with no promises of outcomes or overstated benefits
- Any regulated, legal, or medically risky claim is removed, softened, or flagged for review
- Alt text is provided for all images, if images are included

Fail if any are true:

- Subject line is longer than 50 characters or misleads the reader
- Preview text repeats the subject line or is missing
- More than one CTA competes for attention
- CTA button says “Click here” or “Submit”
- Body copy is too long, hard to scan, or lacks a clear structure
- No clear send trigger, timing, or monthly send context is given
- Sequence emails repeat the same message without progression
- The audience is unclear, or the same copy is used for all audiences without meaningful adaptation
- A monthly nurture email has no clear theme, topical hook, or reason for being sent now
- The resource is mentioned but not named or explained
- A broker email reads as if the broker is the end user, with no shareable client support framing
- A customer email reads like a cold prospecting email
- The copy includes medical advice, diagnosis, or treatment language
- The copy promises results, guarantees outcomes, or makes unsupported health or workplace claims
- Risky wording around prevention, recovery, sickness absence, or product benefit is presented as fact without approval
- Alt text is missing for included images
