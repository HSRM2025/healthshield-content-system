# Legal and Safety Standards

**Governance area:** Legal, compliance, and safety\
**Applies to:** All content outputs across all channels and formats\
**Status:** Active\
**Owner:** Content and Brand team, with input from Compliance and Legal\
**Review cycle:** Annual, or when regulatory requirements change

---

## Purpose

This file sets the legal and safety rules for all Health Shield content. It covers two areas:

1. **General legal and safety standards** — rules that apply to every piece of content we produce
2. **Statutory disclosures** — mandatory wording that must be included exactly as written in specific content types and channels

Use this file alongside:

- `shared/style.md` – style and formatting rules
- `shared/voice.md` – tone and voice guidance
- `governance/consumer-duty-standard.md` – FCA Consumer Duty content requirements

---

## Scope

**Applies to:**

- All member-facing, employer-facing, broker-facing, and public-facing content
- Marketing, UX copy, emails, web, documents, and social media
- AI-generated and human-written content produced under the Health Shield Content System

**Out of scope:**

- FCA-mandated statutory wording where phrasing cannot be altered (this wording must appear exactly as specified in the statutory disclosures section of this file)
- Internal technical documentation where no audience relationship applies

## Hard Rules

### Prohibited claims

1. Do not make guarantees about outcomes such as "will always", "guaranteed to", or "certain to".
2. Do not make unsubstantiated superiority claims such as "best", "leading", "most effective", or "fastest".
3. Do not make health, financial, or legal promises.
4. Do not claim compliance with laws, standards, or regulations unless it has been verified and approved.
5. Do not imply endorsement, approval, or recommendation by a third party unless it is documented and approved.
6. Do not imply that a wellbeing resource on its own will improve health outcomes.
7. Do not promise reduced sickness absence, better retention, higher productivity, or other workplace outcomes unless approved evidence is available.
8. Do not present general wellbeing content as if it were clinical advice or treatment guidance.

### Required disclosures

1. Identify AI-generated content when required by policy, process, or regulation.
2. Disclose data collection at the point of collection.
3. Disclose affiliate, referral, or commercial relationships where relevant.
4. Disclose beta, pilot, or experimental status where relevant.
5. Disclose clear limitations of automated systems where relevant.
6. Make clear when content is general information and not medical advice, where that distinction is needed.
7. Include all product, regulatory, and compliance wording specified in the **Statutory Disclosures** section of this file. This wording is mandatory and must be included exactly as written.

### Liability language

1. Do not use language that removes or limits a person's rights in user-facing content.
2. Do not disclaim responsibility for system or service errors in user-facing copy.
3. Do not shift blame to the reader for problems caused by the system, process, or organisation.
4. Use careful wording for likely outcomes. Prefer "may", "can help", or "can help support" over "will".
5. Do not overstate certainty where outcomes depend on personal, medical, employer, or service factors.

### Regulated health-domain rules

1. Do not diagnose, prescribe, or suggest treatment.
2. Do not imply that general wellbeing information replaces advice from a doctor or other qualified professional.
3. Do not present educational content as personalised medical guidance.
4. Keep health and wellbeing content general, practical, and non-clinical unless approved otherwise.
5. Do not use language that could cause alarm, fear, or pressure people into action.
6. Do not imply that a plan, service, or resource will prevent illness, cure a condition, or improve health for everyone.
7. Take care with language around mental health, neurodiversity, digestive health, self-care, and similar topics. Keep wording supportive and inclusive.
8. If content refers to symptoms, conditions, recovery, prevention, or treatment, it must be reviewed and approved for that use.
9. If content is aimed at employers or brokers, do not turn broad wellbeing support into medical or clinical claims about employees.

### Evidence and substantiation

1. Any factual claim must be true, up to date, and able to be checked.
2. Any performance, outcome, or comparative claim must have approved evidence behind it.
3. If evidence is limited, use careful wording and avoid strong claims.
4. Do not use statistics, rankings, or research findings unless they have been approved for use.
5. Do not turn broad associations into direct promises.
6. If a claim cannot be supported clearly, remove it or send it for review.

### User consent

1. Consent requests must say clearly what the person is agreeing to.
2. Consent must be given through a clear, positive action.
3. Do not use pre-ticked boxes or bundled consent.
4. Consent wording must match the exact scope of the action.
5. It must be as easy to withdraw consent as it is to give it.
6. If content asks someone to share data, sign up, or receive marketing, the reason must be clear.

## Statutory Disclosures

This section contains mandatory wording that must be included exactly as written. Do not paraphrase, shorten, or reword statutory notices. If you are unsure whether a disclosure applies to a piece of content, check with the Compliance team before publishing.

---

### 1. Financial promotions — Health Shield Friendly Society cash plans (regulated products)

**When it applies:** Any content that promotes a Health Shield Friendly Society regulated product, including health cash plans (Corporate Health Cash Plan, Select Health Cash Plan, and others). This includes marketing materials, landing pages, emails, social media, and any content that could constitute a financial promotion.

**Required wording:**

> This is a financial promotion.
>
> Health Shield Friendly Society Limited is authorised by the Prudential Regulation Authority and regulated by the Financial Conduct Authority and the Prudential Regulation Authority.

**Rules:**

- Both lines must appear together. Do not include one without the other.
- Include this notice in the footer or disclosure section of the content. Do not bury it within body copy.
- Do not alter the wording.

---

### 2. Breeze — non-insurance product

**When it applies:** Any content that promotes or describes Breeze products or services. Note: non-insurance business is currently run through Health Shield Friendly Society Limited while the group structure review is being finalised. This notice must be used in the meantime.

**Required wording:**

> This non-insurance product is provided by Health Shield Friendly Society Limited.

**Rules:**

- Include this notice wherever Breeze is promoted as a product or service.
- Review this wording once the group structure review is finalised and update as directed by the Compliance team.
- Do not present Breeze as an insurance product.

---

### 3. Health Checks content — Prevent Ltd trading as Health Shield

**When it applies:** Any content that promotes or describes Health Checks products or services provided by Prevent Ltd trading as Health Shield.

**Required wording:**

> These non-insurance products are provided by Prevent Ltd trading as Health Shield. This company is a subsidiary of Health Shield Wellbeing Limited, a direct subsidiary of Health Shield Friendly Society.

**Rules:**

- Include this notice in any content specifically promoting Health Checks.
- Do not describe Health Checks as an insurance product.
- Use the full legal description of the corporate relationship as written above. Do not abbreviate or summarise it.

---

### 4. Quarterly client emails — mixed HSFS and non-insurance products

**When it applies:** Quarterly client emails that refer to both Health Shield Friendly Society regulated products and non-insurance products in the same communication.

**Required wording:**

> Health Shield Friendly Society Limited is authorised by the Prudential Regulation Authority and regulated by the Financial Conduct Authority and the Prudential Regulation Authority.
>
> Non-insurance products are provided by Prevent Ltd trading as Health Shield. This company is a subsidiary of Health Shield Wellbeing Limited, a direct subsidiary of Health Shield Friendly Society.

**Rules:**

- Both paragraphs must appear together when both product types are referenced in the same email.
- Place this disclosure in the footer of the email.
- If the email refers only to regulated products, use the financial promotions disclosure from section 1 only.
- If the email refers only to non-insurance products, use the Prevent Ltd notice only.

---

### 5. Cashback wording — employee benefit claims

**When it applies:** Any content that mentions the cashback amounts employees can claim through a health cash plan.

**Required wording:**

> The amounts that can be claimed per year depends on the type and level of policy.

**Rules:**

- This notice must appear in close proximity to any reference to cashback amounts or claimable costs.
- Do not state or imply a specific cashback amount without also including this notice.
- This applies across all channels: marketing materials, emails, web copy, and social media.

---

### 6. Online claims — exclusions

**When it applies:** Any content that promotes or describes the ability to make claims online through the Member Account or any digital claims service.

**Required wording:**

> Excludes Maternity, Hospital, Critical Illness and Personal Accident Protection claims.

**Rules:**

- Include this notice wherever online claims are mentioned as a feature or benefit.
- Do not imply that all claims can be made online without including this exclusion.
- The exclusion list is fixed. Do not add to or remove from it without approval from the Compliance team.

---

### 7. Thrive Mental Wellbeing — required description

**When it applies:** Any content that mentions or promotes the Thrive app. The product must always be referred to as **Thrive Mental Wellbeing** and described using the approved wording below.

**Required product name:** Thrive Mental Wellbeing

Do not refer to the product as "Thrive" alone. Always use the full name: **Thrive Mental Wellbeing**.

**Required description (use in full where space allows):**

> **Thrive Mental Wellbeing App**
>
> Thrive Mental Wellbeing is an NHS digitally compliant\* app for the screening and self-management of mental health conditions, such as depression, anxiety and stress. The app contains a range of evidence-based techniques and access to a human-led therapy service.
>
> \*Complies with NHS Digital Technology Assessment Criteria

**Rules:**

- Use the full description wherever the app is described or promoted for the first time in a piece of content.
- The asterisk and footnote explaining NHS Digital Technology Assessment Criteria compliance must always accompany the "NHS digitally compliant" claim.
- Do not make clinical claims about the app beyond those included in the approved description above.
- Do not describe the app as a treatment or as a replacement for professional mental health support.

---

### 8. MyWellness — current wording and phase-out note

**When it applies:** Any content that mentions MyWellness benefits or services.

**Phase-out status:** Health Shield is in the process of phasing out the use of the "MyWellness" name. Check with the Content and Brand team before creating new content that leads with this name. Where existing references remain, the notices below must be included.

**Required notices (include where MyWellness services are listed or promoted):**

> The services available on MyWellness may differ according to the type of plan. Services and information available on MyWellness can change without notice.

**Rules:**

- Include both sentences together whenever MyWellness services are listed or described.
- Do not guarantee which services are available under a given plan. Always include the notice that services may differ.
- As the MyWellness name is phased out, content should be updated to reflect the replacement naming once confirmed. Do not create new content that centres the MyWellness name without checking the current status with the Content and Brand team.

---

### 9. Treatment coverage — health cash plans

**When it applies:** Any content that mentions types of treatments or healthcare costs covered by a health cash plan.

**Required wording:**

> \*Please refer to your plan's terms and conditions before receiving any treatment. The amounts that can be claimed per year depends on the type and level of policy.

**Rules:**

- Include this notice wherever treatment types or covered costs are listed or described.
- The asterisk should link to, or appear alongside, the relevant plan terms and conditions where possible.
- Do not imply that all treatments are covered without condition. Always direct readers to check their plan terms before proceeding.

## Prohibited Patterns

| Pattern                                                  | Issue                        | Better approach                                                    |
| -------------------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| `This will improve employee health`                      | Health outcome promise       | `This may help support everyday wellbeing`                         |
| `Guaranteed results`                                     | Outcome promise              | `Designed to help`                                                 |
| `This toolkit will reduce sickness absence`              | Unsupported workplace claim  | `This resource offers practical ways to support wellbeing at work` |
| `Prevents illness`                                       | Prevention claim             | Remove or send for review                                          |
| `Treats anxiety`                                         | Treatment claim              | Remove or send for review                                          |
| `Just follow these tips`                                 | Oversimplifies health topic  | `Here are some simple ideas that may help`                         |
| `By continuing you agree`                                | Passive consent              | Use a clear consent action                                         |
| `Trust us`                                               | Unsupported reassurance      | State the specific support, safeguard, or limitation               |
| Referring to Thrive as just "Thrive"                     | Incomplete product name      | Always use "Thrive Mental Wellbeing"                               |
| Claiming all treatments are covered                      | Inaccurate, no qualification | Include treatment coverage notice from section 9                   |
| Implying all claims can be made online                   | Inaccurate, no exclusion     | Include online claims exclusion from section 6                     |
| Stating a specific cashback amount without qualification | Misleading without context   | Include cashback wording from section 5                            |

## Safer Wording for Health and Wellbeing Content

**Prefer:**

- may help
- can help support
- designed to help
- practical advice
- general wellbeing information
- everyday support
- helpful resource

**Avoid:**

- will improve
- fixes
- cures
- prevents
- guarantees
- treatment
- prescription
- medically proven

---

## Interaction with Skills

1. All skills inherit these rules.

2. Skills that cover health, wellbeing, benefits, regulated services, or consent must apply this file by default.

3. Skills must not override the ban on diagnosis, prescription, unsupported claims, or misleading certainty.

4. Skills must not override or omit mandatory statutory disclosure wording.

5. Rubrics for relevant skills should check for:

   - unsupported claims
   - medical or clinical wording
   - missing or altered statutory disclosures
   - risky workplace-outcome promises
   - unclear consent language
   - incorrect product naming (especially Thrive Mental Wellbeing)

6. If a skill produces content in a higher-risk area, it should direct the user to review or approval where needed.

---

## Quick check

Before approving content, make sure it:

- does not diagnose or prescribe
- does not present general wellbeing content as medical advice
- does not promise health or workplace outcomes without approved evidence
- uses careful wording such as "may" or "can help support"
- keeps claims measured and clear
- includes all required statutory disclosures exactly as written
- uses the correct product names (especially "Thrive Mental Wellbeing" in full)
- includes cashback, online claims, and treatment coverage notices wherever triggered
- includes MyWellness service variation notices where applicable
- is sent for review if any claim or wording feels risky
