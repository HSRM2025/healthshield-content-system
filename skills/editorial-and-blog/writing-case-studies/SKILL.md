# Writing case studies

## Quick start

Collect or infer:

- Customer name, industry, size, and work environment
- The challenge they faced before their health cash plan
- How important their people's wellbeing is to them
- How a health cash plan helped, and which benefits matter most
- Results achieved (quantified where possible) and any employee stories
- Story angle (the warm, human theme we're telling)
- Interview format (relaxed Zoom call or on-site session with a film crew)
- Approval status across two stages (see Workflow)
- Approved channels (website, LinkedIn, YouTube, ad landing pages, eBooks and brochures)
- Approved repurposing rights (shorter clips, quote cards, stills, captions, one-pagers)
- Target audience for the story

Then produce output using [TEMPLATES.md](https://TEMPLATES.md). Validate with [RUBRIC.md](https://RUBRIC.md).

## Workflow

1. Run the pre-outreach go/no-go check (customer happy, no open issues, real results yet, contact still in role, no reference restrictions, scope clear).
2. Reach out warmly, with a no-pressure invitation and upfront clarity on how the story may be used.
3. Share the pre-interview brief, and agree the interview format (Zoom or on-site).
4. Run the interview using the question bank in [reference/interview-question-bank.md](https://reference/interview-question-bank.md). Pick the prompts that suit the story.
5. Gather raw materials (interview transcript, metrics, customer quotes, logo, supporting imagery).
6. Select structure from [reference/case-study-structure.md](https://reference/case-study-structure.md).
7. Identify the narrative arc: challenge → solution → the difference it made.
8. Write a warm, specific headline with the result or transformation.
9. Draft each section, leading with real, human specifics over generalities.
10. Pull and attribute quotes throughout, in the customer's own words (not just at the end).
11. Quantify results wherever you can (absences, engagement, claims), while keeping claims clear, fair, and not misleading.
12. Add visual element placeholders (logo, team photos, supporting imagery).
13. **Stage 1 – editorial approval:** get the customer to sign off the words and the angle (draft, quotes, name and title, metrics).
14. Once the words are approved, design the finished assets.
15. **Stage 2 – asset and usage approval:** get the customer to sign off the polished assets and tell us exactly which channels and repurposing rights they're happy with.
16. Store the signed release and usage form centrally, and update the publication log with live links.
17. Set the next review date (at least every 2 years), with immediate review triggers if the contact leaves, branding changes, metrics age, or a new channel is needed.
18. Run the rubric check. Revise until it passes.

## Degrees of freedom

- **Low**: Must follow the two-stage approval process; must include a quantified result (or a clear note when numbers aren't shared) and an attributed customer quote; must follow the challenge-solution-difference arc; must keep claims clear, fair, and not misleading.
- **Medium**: Structure format, quote placement, narrative voice, choice of interview questions.
- Allowed variation: Long vs. short format, single-page vs. multi-page, video vs. written, broad vs. narrow channel use – as long as the rubric passes and the customer has happily approved the scope.
