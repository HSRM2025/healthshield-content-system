# `market-survey-whitepaper` — RUBRIC.md

## Purpose

Use this rubric to review whether a **market-facing, gated, PR-supporting survey results whitepaper** is ready to publish.

It is designed for Health Shield teams and AI reviewers who need to check that a draft is:

- true to the campaign objective
- accurate and well-evidenced
- easy to quote and easy to scan
- safe from a compliance point of view
- in line with Health Shield’s tone and brand
- complete enough for gated publication and PR use

This is a **quality-control document**. It is here to help reviewers spot risk early, make decisions with confidence, and make sure the final whitepaper feels helpful, polished, and trustworthy.

---

## How to use this rubric

Review the whitepaper against each dimension below.

For each dimension, mark one of these outcomes:

- **Pass** – meets the standard
- **Needs work** – usable direction, but changes are needed before sign-off
- **Fail** – does not meet the standard

### Review order

Use this order when reviewing:

1. Check for **critical failures**

2. Review each evaluation dimension

3. Confirm all required end matter is present

4. Make a final decision:

   - Pass
   - Revise and review again
   - Fail

> \[!IMPORTANT]\
> If the draft contains a critical failure, it cannot pass, even if other areas are strong.

---

## Pass conditions

A whitepaper can pass when:

- it meets the campaign goal clearly
- the narrative is coherent and useful
- all key findings are accurate and properly framed as survey findings
- the strongest points are easy for journalists to lift
- the writing is clear, calm, and easy to scan
- methodology and attribution are present and correct
- subgroup and year-on-year claims are careful and supported
- it follows all guardrails
- brand mentions are measured
- product mentions stay soft and non-promotional
- it is suitable for gated publication and PR support
- all required end matter is complete
- no critical failures are present

---

## Fail conditions

A whitepaper fails when one or more of these are true:

- it contains any critical failure
- the central story is unclear or weak
- statistics are wrong, unsupported, or overstated
- findings are presented as universal fact
- the draft reads more like promotion than an insight-led report
- methodology is missing where required
- the report is not safe for PR use
- the required regulatory wording is missing or changed
- the draft would need major rewriting, not just editing

---

## Rating scale

| Rating     | Meaning                          | Action                       |
| ---------- | -------------------------------- | ---------------------------- |
| Pass       | Ready for next stage or sign-off | Proceed                      |
| Needs work | Strong base, but changes needed  | Revise and re-review         |
| Fail       | Not suitable in current form     | Rework before further review |

---

## Evaluation dimensions

## 1. Strategic fit to campaign objective

Checks whether the whitepaper is clearly tied to the intended campaign and audience.

| Criteria            | Pass                                                                 | Needs work                                                 | Fail                                            |
| ------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------- |
| Campaign alignment  | The report clearly supports the agreed campaign objective            | The objective is partly visible, but not strong throughout | The report feels disconnected from the campaign |
| Audience relevance  | The report speaks clearly to the intended external audience          | Some sections feel too broad or internal                   | The audience is unclear or poorly served        |
| Market-facing value | The report offers useful external insight, not just internal summary | Insight is present, but the value is uneven                | The report does not feel market-facing          |
| Gated asset value   | The report feels substantial enough to be worth downloading          | The content has some value, but lacks depth or shape       | The report does not feel worth gating           |

**Pass rule:** All four criteria should be at least **Pass** or **Needs work**, with no more than one **Needs work**.

---

## 2. Strength of narrative arc

Checks whether the report tells a clear story rather than listing findings.

| Criteria                   | Pass                                                          | Needs work                                     | Fail                                          |
| -------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------- |
| Clear central message      | There is one clear main story running through the report      | The story is present, but diluted              | No clear story emerges                        |
| Chapter flow               | Sections build naturally from one to the next                 | Some chapter order or transitions need work    | The structure feels disjointed                |
| Use of tension and meaning | The report shows what the findings reveal and why they matter | Meaning is present, but some sections are flat | The report reads like a data dump             |
| Ending strength            | The report closes with clarity and purpose                    | The ending is serviceable, but weak            | The report ends abruptly or without direction |

**Pass rule:** Narrative must feel coherent from start to finish.

---

## 3. Accuracy and use of survey findings

Checks whether the report uses survey evidence correctly.

| Criteria             | Pass                                         | Needs work                                    | Fail                                         |
| -------------------- | -------------------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------- |
| Statistical accuracy | All figures match approved source material   | Minor checks needed, but no clear inaccuracy  | Any statistic is wrong or cannot be verified |
| Use of findings      | Findings are used carefully and meaningfully | Some findings are under-explained or overused | Findings are misused or distorted            |
| Evidence-led writing | Claims follow from the data                  | Some claims need tighter wording              | Claims go beyond what the data supports      |
| Framing of findings  | Findings are framed as survey findings       | Some phrasing needs softening or attribution  | Findings are presented as universal fact     |

**Pass rule:** No inaccurate or unsupported claims. Any error in stats is an automatic **Fail** for this dimension.

---

## 4. Journalist-friendly quotability

Checks whether the whitepaper supports PR use and easy media lift.

| Criteria         | Pass                                                                        | Needs work                                      | Fail                                       |
| ---------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------ |
| Headline stats   | Key figures are easy to spot and stand alone clearly                        | Useful stats are present, but not surfaced well | Strong stats are buried or unclear         |
| Quotable lines   | The report includes crisp, liftable lines that stay accurate out of context | Some lines are usable, but need tightening      | The report lacks quotable material         |
| Media usefulness | Findings are presented in a way journalists can pick up quickly             | Some sections are too dense or slow             | The report is hard to lift from for PR use |
| Tone control     | Wording is strong but not sensational                                       | A few lines lean too hard into drama            | The report is exaggerated or alarmist      |

**Pass rule:** The draft should contain clear media-ready material without losing accuracy.

---

## 5. Clarity and scanability

Checks whether the report is easy to read and easy to navigate.

| Criteria                 | Pass                                                   | Needs work                                       | Fail                                                 |
| ------------------------ | ------------------------------------------------------ | ------------------------------------------------ | ---------------------------------------------------- |
| Plain language           | Language is clear, simple, and easy to follow          | Some copy feels too formal or heavy              | The report is hard to understand                     |
| Paragraph length         | Paragraphs are short and manageable                    | Some sections are dense                          | The report is full of long blocks of text            |
| Headings and subheadings | Section labels help readers move through the report    | Some headings need sharpening                    | Headings are vague, inconsistent, or missing         |
| Scannable structure      | Bullets, callouts, and sectioning support easy reading | Structure is usable, but could be easier to scan | The report is visually and structurally hard to scan |

**Pass rule:** The report should work for both close reading and quick scanning.

---

## 6. Methodology and attribution

Checks whether the report explains the research properly and attributes findings correctly.

| Criteria              | Pass                                                     | Needs work                        | Fail                                         |
| --------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------- | --------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------- |
| Methodology included  | A methodology section is present where needed            | Present, but incomplete           | Missing                                      |
| Core research details | Sample, fieldwork, commissioner, and method are included | One or two details need adding    | Key research details are missing             |
| Attribution           | Findings are clearly attributed to the survey or report  | Some attribution needs tightening | Findings are unattributed or loosely claimed |
| Comparability wording | Any year-on-year caveat is included where needed         | Caveat is present, but unclear    | Comparisons are used without needed context  |

**Pass rule:** Methodology and attribution must be good enough to support external credibility.

---

## 7. Handling of subgroup and year-on-year claims

Checks whether the report handles nuance carefully.

| Criteria              | Pass                                                             | Needs work                           | Fail                                          |
| --------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------ | --------------------------------------------- |
| Subgroup accuracy     | Subgroup findings match approved inputs                          | Some subgroup wording needs checking | Subgroup claims are unsupported or inaccurate |
| Subgroup restraint    | Subgroup insights add value without overclaiming                 | Some points are stretched            | Differences are overstated                    |
| Year-on-year validity | Comparisons are only used where approved and valid               | Some comparison wording needs care   | Invalid or misleading comparisons are made    |
| Interpretation        | Differences are described carefully, not as proof of broad truth | Some statements need softening       | Claims are too absolute or causal             |

**Pass rule:** Any subgroup or comparison claim must be supportable and carefully worded.

---

## 8. Compliance with guardrails

Checks whether the report stays within Health Shield’s non-negotiables.

| Criteria                           | Pass                                                       | Needs work                           | Fail                                            |
| ---------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------ | ----------------------------------------------- |
| Nationally representative phrasing | No such phrasing appears                                   | Borderline wording needs changing    | Any such phrasing appears                       |
| Competitor references              | None appear                                                | Indirect reference needs removing    | Any competitor reference appears                |
| Advice-like language               | The draft avoids legal, medical, and regulated-advice tone | A few lines need softening           | The draft sounds like advice in regulated areas |
| Required regulatory wording        | Included exactly as approved                               | Present but placement needs checking | Missing or altered                              |

**Pass rule:** All four criteria must be **Pass**.

---

## 9. Brand fit and tone

Checks whether the whitepaper sounds like Health Shield without becoming promotional.

| Criteria               | Pass                                                   | Needs work                       | Fail                                     |
| ---------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------ | -------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------- |
| Warmth and trust       | The tone feels calm, supportive, and clear             | Tone is mostly right, but uneven | Tone feels cold, corporate, or off-brand |
| Professionalism        | The report feels credible and careful                  | Some sections need tightening    | Tone feels sloppy, pushy, or unsuitable  |
| Reader focus           | The writing stays focused on audience need and insight | Some sections drift inward       | The report feels self-focused            |
| Conversational clarity | The style is natural and digestible                    | Some wording is too formal       | The style is stiff or jargon-heavy       |

**Pass rule:** The report should feel helpful and grounded from start to finish.

---

## 10. Softness of product mentions

Checks whether product and brand references stay measured.

| Criteria              | Pass                                          | Needs work                                        | Fail                                 |
| --------------------- | --------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------ |
| Product restraint     | Product mentions are light-touch and relevant | One or two mentions feel too forward              | Product promotion shapes the report  |
| Insight-first balance | The report leads with findings, not solutions | Some passages lean too quickly into solution mode | The report reads like a brochure     |
| Sales tone            | No hard-sell wording appears                  | Some wording needs softening                      | The copy is promotional or sales-led |
| Brand role            | Health Shield appears as a helpful guide      | Brand presence is slightly heavy                  | Brand presence dominates the report  |

**Pass rule:** The whitepaper must remain insight-led.

---

## 11. Suitability for gated publication and PR support

Checks whether the draft is strong enough for both download and media use.

| Criteria        | Pass                                                        | Needs work                                            | Fail                                 |
| --------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------ |
| Download value  | The report feels worth exchanging details for               | Some value is present, but depth is limited           | The report does not feel substantial |
| PR readiness    | The draft supports media, press release, and campaign reuse | Some sections need stronger hooks or cleaner phrasing | Not suitable for PR support          |
| Reusability     | Key lines and sections can be adapted for other channels    | Some useful material exists, but not enough           | The content is hard to reuse         |
| External polish | The overall draft feels publication-ready in tone and shape | Promising, but still rough                            | Not ready for public-facing use      |

**Pass rule:** The report should feel useful both as a lead asset and as a source for wider campaign content.

---

## 12. Completeness of required end matter

Checks whether the final sections needed for publication are present and complete.

| Required end matter | Pass standard                     | Fail standard         |
| ------------------- | --------------------------------- | --------------------- |
| Contents page       | Present and matches headings      | Missing or inaccurate |
| Methodology         | Present where required            | Missing               |
| Citation block      | Present and usable                | Missing or incomplete |
| Media enquiries     | Present if PR support is required | Missing where needed  |
| About Health Shield | Present and approved              | Missing or unapproved |
| Regulatory wording  | Present exactly as required       | Missing or changed    |

**Pass rule:** All required end matter must be present before final approval.

---

## 13. Stylistic variety and natural flow

Checks whether the draft report varies its sentence structures, openings, transitions, and section endings.

| Criteria | Pass | Needs work | Fail |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Sentence variety | Sentence structures feel varied and natural across the report | Some sentence patterns are repeated too often | The same sentence formulas recur throughout the report |
| Opening variety | Sections and chapters open in different ways, with no obvious repeated setup | Some openings feel too similar | Most sections open with the same type of sentence or framing |
| Transition control | Transitions are light-touch, varied, and not overused | Some transition phrases are repeated or over-signposted | Repeated transition phrases make the draft feel mechanical or over-signposted |
| Ending variety | Sections end in ways that feel purposeful but not uniform | Some endings feel repetitive or over-shaped | Most sections close with the same takeaway, business-case line, or summary pattern |
| Formula restraint | Stock constructions such as “By X, organisations can Y” or “not just X, but Y” are used sparingly or not at all | One or two formulaic constructions are overused | Repeated formulaic constructions make the report sound templated or AI-assisted |
| Structural variation | Chapters do not all follow the same challenge–implication–response pattern | Some chapters feel too similarly built | The report feels mechanically assembled from repeated section shapes |
| Business-case restraint | Commercial or organisational implications are used where genuinely helpful, not attached to every point | Some findings are given unnecessary business framing | Nearly every issue is converted into a business case, weakening nuance and variety |

**Pass rule:** The report should read as varied and naturally written from start to finish. Repeated sentence formulas, over-signposting, or mechanically repeated section shapes should result in **Needs work** or **Fail**, depending on severity.

---

## Critical failures

Any item below is an automatic fail for the draft until fixed.

### Automatic fail list

- wording that claims or implies findings are **nationally representative**
- any **competitor reference**
- **hard-sell** or brochure-style promotion
- wording that sounds like **legal, medical, or regulated advice**
- required regulatory wording is **missing, incomplete, or altered**
- **unsupported claims**
- **inaccurate statistics**
- **missing methodology** where methodology is required
- presenting survey findings as **universal fact**
- implying **causation** where the data does not support it

### Suggested decision rule

- **Pass**\
  No critical failures, and all dimensions are **Pass** or only a small number are **Needs work** with minor edits.
- **Revise and review again**\
  No critical failures, but several dimensions are **Needs work**, or one important dimension needs meaningful revision.
- **Fail**\
  Any critical failure is present, or the draft has multiple failed dimensions that would require major rewriting.

---

## Final decision guidance

## Pass

Choose **Pass** when the report:

- is accurate
- feels joined-up and useful
- is easy to quote
- is safe for compliance review or final sign-off
- is ready for gated publication and PR support

## Revise and review again

Choose this when the report has a strong base, but still needs work in areas such as:

- shaping the story
- tightening claims
- improving quotability
- making the structure easier to scan
- softening brand or product language
- filling in end matter

## Fail

Choose **Fail** when the report:

- breaks any non-negotiable rule
- misuses evidence
- is not safe for external publication
- lacks a clear story
- would need major rewriting to become usable

- [ ] The regulatory wording appears exactly as approved

---

## Notes for reviewers

If you are unsure between **Needs work** and **Fail**, ask this:

**Can this be fixed with editing, or does it need rewriting?**

- If it can be fixed with careful editing, mark **Needs work**
- If the issue affects trust, accuracy, compliance, or the whole shape of the report, mark **Fail**

When in doubt, choose care over speed. A good whitepaper should feel clear, helpful, and safe to stand behind.
