# Rubric

Pass if all are true:
- Within platform character limit
- First line is a hook (earns attention, not filler)
- Core message is clear without clicking links
- CTA matches post goal (engagement gets question, traffic gets link)
- Tone matches brand voice and platform norms
- Hashtags are relevant and within platform conventions
- Links are functional and lead to expected destination
- No broken formatting (line breaks render correctly)
- the writing sounds natural and specific rather than templated
- sentence lengths vary in a way that feels human and easy to read
- conclusions feel earned and relevant
- transitions are used sparingly and only where helpful


Fail if any are true:
- Exceeds character limit for platform
- Opens with weak phrasing ("We're excited to announce...", "Check out...")
- Buries the value below the fold (Instagram) or after click-through
- CTA is missing when post has a goal beyond awareness
- Hashtag stuffing (>5 on LinkedIn, >3 on Twitter, >30 on Instagram)
- Uses hashtags that are off-topic or excessively broad (#success #motivation)
- Tone mismatch (corporate speak on casual platform, casual on LinkedIn thought leadership)
- Thread doesn't have clear structure or payoff
- Contains unverified claims or promises
- @mentions people/brands without relevance
- the draft relies on contrast-by-negation patterns such as "isn't just X, it's Y"
- the copy uses rhetorical kicker lines or ask-then-answer formulas without a clear reason
- buzzwords or inflated phrasing replace plain English
- the ending is generic, moralising, or inspirational without substance
- the rhythm is uniformly flat across the piece
