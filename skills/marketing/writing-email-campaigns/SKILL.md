# Writing Email Campaigns

## Quick start

Collect or infer:

- Campaign goal – nurture, engage, convert, retain, re-engage, or support
- Audience segment – prospect, broker, customer, member, employer, or internal audience where relevant
- Relationship stage – new lead, active opportunity, existing customer, lapsed contact, or ongoing nurture
- Email type – single send, sequence, or recurring monthly nurture email
- Monthly theme or campaign theme – for example social wellbeing, gut health, financial wellbeing, or an awareness moment
- Featured resource type – toolkit, tipsheet, guide, checklist, webinar, article, or landing page
- Key message and desired action
- Primary CTA – one action only
- Send cadence or trigger
- Compliance considerations for health-related content, regulated claims, and approved product language


Then produce output using [TEMPLATES.md](TEMPLATES.md). Validate with [RUBRIC.md](RUBRIC.md).

## Workflow

1. Define the campaign goal and success measure

2. Identify the audience segment and relationship stage

3. Confirm whether this is a single send, a sequence, or a monthly nurture email

4. For monthly nurture emails, define the monthly theme, seasonal hook, or awareness moment

5. Confirm the featured resource type and the exact asset being promoted

6. Adapt the message for the audience:

   - prospects – lead with practical value and light nurture
   - brokers – frame as useful content they can share with clients
   - customers – focus on added value and helping them get more from the relationship

7. Write the subject line and preview text so they are clear, useful, and relevant

8. Draft body copy around one main message and one primary CTA

9. Check that health-related language stays educational, careful, and non-clinical

10. Review for compliance, brand terms, and any claim-risk wording

11. Run the rubric check and revise until it passes

## Degrees of freedom

- **Medium**: Email structure should stay consistent, but tone, framing, and message emphasis can change by audience, theme, and campaign type
- Allowed variation: Single send or sequence format; send timing; resource-led structure; seasonal framing; audience-specific proof points

## Failure modes to avoid

- Generic copy that does not reflect the audience segment
- Writing broker emails as if brokers are the end user rather than the sharer
- Writing customer emails as if they are cold prospects
- Overly promotional copy that weakens the value-led nurture approach
- Multiple CTAs competing for attention
- Subject lines or preview text that mislead about the content
- Walls of text that bury the key message
- Repeating the same message across a sequence without progression
- Missing or unclear monthly theme in recurring nurture emails
- Medical, diagnostic, or guaranteed-outcome language in health-related content
- Unapproved claims about health benefits, workplace outcomes, or product performance
