# Health Cash Plan Copy Review

**Governance area:** Review processes\
**File path:** `governance/review-processes/health-cash-plan-copy-review.md`\
**Applies to:** Marketing review of employer-submitted Health Cash Plan web copy\
**Status:** Active\
**Owner:** Content and Brand team\
**Review cycle:** Annual

---

## Purpose

This document sets out the internal process for reviewing web copy submitted by employers for their Health Cash Plan scheme pages. It defines what the marketing team reviews, what it does not review, and how review decisions are recorded and communicated.

This is a marketing and content review process. It is not a product validation or compliance sign-off process.

---

## Scope

**Applies to:**

- Web copy submitted by employers for live Health Cash Plan schemes
- Copy intended for publication on employer-facing or member-facing digital channels
- AI-assisted and human-written reviews conducted under the Health Shield Content System

**Does not apply to:**

- Internal scheme documentation
- PDF benefit guides
- Printed materials
- Copy for products other than the Health Cash Plan range

---

## When this process applies

Use this process when:

- An employer has submitted a draft of web copy for their Health Cash Plan scheme page
- The copy is intended to go live on a Health Shield or employer-hosted digital channel
- Marketing has been asked to review the copy before publication

Do not begin the review until all required inputs are confirmed as present. If inputs are missing, return the submission to the employer with a list of what is needed before review can start.

---

## Required inputs

The following must be present before a review begins:

| Input                                 | Notes                                                     |
| ------------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------- |
| Employer draft copy                   | Full text of the web page or section under review         |
| Scheme name                           | As it should appear on the page                           |
| Cover levels                          | All tiers or plan options included in the scheme          |
| Employer or employee paid             | Confirm which applies, or both                            |
| Dependent children covered            | Confirm yes or no                                         |
| Contact details or next steps         | URL, phone number, or internal process for Members to act |
| Any scheme-specific eligibility rules | Waiting periods, employee categories, exclusions          |

If any of these are missing or marked as placeholder, do not approve the copy until they are confirmed by the employer.

---

## Review framework

All copy must be reviewed against the following shared standards. Each standard file is the source of truth for its area.

| Standard                  | What it governs                                                                |
| ------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| `shared/style.md`         | Capitalisation, punctuation, formatting, sentence structure                    |
| `shared/voice.md`         | Voice attributes, tone, person, and how we address Members                     |
| `shared/accessibility.md` | Reading level, inclusive language, cognitive load, screen reader compatibility |
| `shared/legal-safety.md`  | Regulated wording, prohibited claims, required disclosures                     |

Apply all four standards in every review. No single standard takes precedence over another.

---

## What marketing reviews

The content review covers the following:

**Style and formatting**

- Sentence case applied correctly throughout
- "Members" capitalised wherever used
- Product names in title case (e.g. Corporate Health Cash Plan, Select Health Cash Plan)
- "Health Shield" written as two words, no variants
- Em dash not used; en dash used correctly with spaces where needed
- Punctuation consistent and correct
- Paragraphs kept to three sentences or fewer
- Bullet point lists formatted consistently

**Voice and tone**

- Copy is warm, clear, and written for a reading age of 9 to 11
- Active voice used as the default
- Contractions used where natural (we're, you've, we'll)
- Formal or corporate language replaced with plain English alternatives (see `governance/friendly-language-standard.md`)
- Exclamation marks used only in direct member communications, not in general web copy

**Accessibility**

- Plain English used throughout; no unexplained jargon
- Link text is descriptive, not generic ("Find out more about your plan" not "Click here")
- No directional language that would confuse assistive technology users
- Reading level appropriate for the audience

**Legal and safety**

- No absolute or unqualified claims (e.g. "covers everything", "guaranteed", "always")
- FCA-sensitive language handled correctly
- Required disclosures present where applicable
- No prohibited claims as defined in `shared/legal-safety.md`

## What is out of scope for marketing

The following are outside the marketing review. Do not validate, amend, or approve these items:

- Specific benefit values, cash limits, or claim amounts
- Financial figures of any kind
- Scheme eligibility rules or waiting periods
- Policy numbers or scheme reference codes
- Employer-specific URLs, email addresses, or phone numbers
- Cover details unique to the employer's scheme configuration

If copy contains any of the above and it appears incomplete, incorrect, or inconsistent, flag it to the employer. Do not correct it directly.

---

## What marketing can correct directly

Minor content corrections can be made directly without returning copy to the employer:

- Typographical errors and spelling mistakes
- Apostrophe errors
- Inconsistent or incorrect capitalisation against HSCS style rules
- Missing contractions where the voice standard requires them
- Punctuation errors (commas, full stops, colons)
- Sentences that are too long and can be split without changing meaning
- Bullet point list formatting

When making direct corrections, record all changes in the review note output so the employer can see what was changed and why.

---

## What must be flagged back to the employer

Return copy to the employer for confirmation or correction when:

- Scheme-specific details are missing, incomplete, or appear as unfilled placeholders
- Financial figures, benefit limits, or claim amounts are present but cannot be verified by marketing
- Eligibility rules or scheme conditions are unclear or contradictory
- Contact details, URLs, or next-step instructions are missing or placeholder
- Copy makes a claim about scheme coverage that marketing cannot confirm is accurate
- Regulated wording appears to be missing or incorrectly applied

Flag items clearly in the review note. State what is missing or unclear and what the employer needs to supply or confirm before the copy can be approved.

---

## Approval statuses

Use one of the following four statuses in every review output:

| Status                                        | Meaning                                                                                                                       |
| --------------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Approved**                                  | Copy meets all standards. No changes needed. Ready to publish.                                                                |
| **Approved with edits**                       | Copy meets standards after direct corrections made by marketing. Changes recorded in review note. Ready to publish.           |
| **Approved subject to employer confirmation** | Copy is broadly compliant but contains items that must be confirmed by the employer before publication. Not ready to publish. |
| **Not ready**                                 | Copy does not meet standards and requires significant revision. Return to employer with full feedback.                        |

---

## Escalation triggers

Escalate to the compliance team immediately if:

- Copy appears to make a regulated financial claim without the required disclosure
- Copy suggests Health Shield guarantees a health or financial outcome
- Copy references FCA-regulated products or services in a way that appears non-compliant
- Copy has been submitted for a scheme that is not yet live or confirmed as active

Do not approve or publish copy that meets any escalation trigger until compliance has reviewed it.

---

## Output format for review notes

Every completed review must produce a review note. Use the following structure:

```
## Health Cash Plan Copy Review

**Employer:** [Employer name]
**Scheme:** [Scheme name]
**Review date:** [DD Month YYYY]
**Reviewer:** [Name or role]
**Status:** [Approved / Approved with edits / Approved subject to employer confirmation / Not ready]

---

### Direct corrections made
List each correction with a brief reason. If none, write: None.

### Items flagged to employer
List each flagged item with a clear explanation of what is missing or needs confirmation. If none, write: None.

### Escalation
State whether anything was escalated to compliance. If none, write: None.

### Notes
Any additional context relevant to this review.
```

Store completed review notes in the relevant employer folder. Do not publish copy until the review note status is Approved or Approved with edits.
