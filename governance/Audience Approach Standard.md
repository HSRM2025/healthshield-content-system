# Audience Approach Standard

**Governance area:** Audience and communication\
**Applies to:** All customer-facing, member-facing, and employer-facing content\
**Status:** Active\
**Owner:** Content and Brand team\
**Review cycle:** Annual, or following significant audience or regulatory change

---

## Purpose

This standard sets out how Health Shield tailors its communication to different audiences. It exists to make sure every piece of content we produce puts people first – their needs, their questions, and their wellbeing – rather than leading with products and features.

Use this standard alongside `shared/voice.md`, `shared/style.md`, and `governance/friendly-language-standard.md`.

---

## Scope

**Applies to:**

- All content written on behalf of Health Shield, including marketing, email, web, member communications, and employer-facing materials
- All content creators, including in-house writers, agency partners, and AI-assisted workflows

**Out of scope:**

- Internal-only documents where no audience relationship applies
- Regulated disclosure text where FCA-mandated wording cannot be altered

---

## Definitions

| Term                      | Meaning in this context                                                                                                            |
| ------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| B2B audience              | Employers – HR leads, decision-makers, and business owners who purchase or manage Health Shield plans on behalf of their workforce |
| D2C audience              | Employees and members – the individuals who use Health Shield plans and interact with our services directly                        |
| Customer-centric language | Writing that leads with the audience's needs, concerns, and outcomes rather than product features or commercial objectives         |
| Tell, don't sell          | An approach where we identify a customer need and offer a relevant solution, without resorting to promotional or sales-led framing |

## 1. Customer-centred approach

### 1.1 Core principle

Health Shield's tone of voice stays consistent across all content – warm, clear, and human. But the way we apply that tone shifts depending on who we are talking to and what they need from us.

Before writing anything, ask: *who is this for, and what do they need to know or feel?* That question should shape every word.

### 1.2 Our two main audiences

**Employers (B2B)**

Employers are often time-pressed and outcome-focused. They need to understand how Health Shield helps their business and their people. They care about:

- Supporting employee wellbeing and retention
- Getting value from their investment
- Simple, clear information that helps them make good decisions
- Compliance with their responsibilities as employers

When writing for employers, lead with the impact on their team and their workplace. Show you understand the pressures they face. Then offer Health Shield as a practical, caring partner – not just a product provider.

**Employees and members (D2C)**

Members are often dealing with everyday health concerns, costs, and questions. They may not always know what they're entitled to or how to get help. They need to feel:

- Heard and supported
- Confident that things will be sorted out quickly
- That Health Shield is on their side

When writing for members, be warm and direct. Use plain language. Make the next step obvious. Remove anything that could feel confusing or cold.

### 1.3 Lead with empathy, not products

Instead of opening with what we offer, start with what the audience is experiencing. Identify the challenge or need first, then introduce the solution.

**Avoid:**

> "Our Corporate Health Cash Plan lets employees claim back costs for dental, optical, and physiotherapy treatment."

**Prefer:**

> "Everyday health costs add up. With a health cash plan, your employees can get money back on things like dental check-ups, eye tests, and physio – so those costs don't have to get in the way of their wellbeing."

The facts are the same. But the second version speaks to a real concern before offering the answer.

### 1.4 Tell, don't sell

Identifying a customer need is an opportunity to offer a solution – not to push a sale. Our job is to give people the information and confidence they need to make the right choice for themselves. That means being helpful and honest, not promotional.

**In practice, this means:**

- Start with the person's situation, not our product
- Explain what they can do or get, not just what we provide
- Use language that feels supportive, not persuasive
- Avoid urgency tactics or commercial pressure
- Let the value speak for itself

### 1.5 Practical guidance for writers

**Before writing, ask:**

1. Who is the primary reader of this content?
2. What do they need to know, do, or feel after reading it?
3. What challenge or concern is this content helping them with?
4. Am I leading with their need, or with our product?

**When writing:**

- Use "you" and "your" to speak directly to the reader
- Keep sentences short and paragraphs to three lines or fewer
- Put the most important information first
- Use bullet points to make key details easy to scan
- Avoid jargon, and never assume the reader knows how our products work
- If a decision or action is needed, make it clear and simple

**Language shifts by audience:**

| B2B writing                                   | D2C writing                                           |
| --------------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------- |
| Slightly more formal, but still warm          | More conversational and reassuring                    |
| Focus on business outcomes and team wellbeing | Focus on personal health and everyday support         |
| May reference HR, workplace policy, and ROI   | Avoids workplace or corporate framing                 |
| "Your employees" or "your team"               | "You" and "your family"                               |
| Exclamation marks used sparingly              | Exclamation marks acceptable in direct member content |

## 2. Mandatory requirements

The following apply to all audience-facing content regardless of channel or format:

- **Writers shall** identify the primary audience before drafting any content
- **Writers shall** lead with the audience's need or concern, not with a product or feature
- **Writers shall** use the language preferences set out in `governance/friendly-language-standard.md`
- **Writers shall not** use sales-led framing in content aimed at members or employees
- **Writers shall** ensure that any required FCA disclosures are included where relevant, in line with `shared/legal-safety.md`
